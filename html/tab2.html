What is the Dark matter?

Dark matter is a type of matter present in the universe that does not emit light and cannot be directly detected, although its presence can be inferred from the gravitational effects it exerts on other celestial bodies. This invisible matter makes up about 84% of the matter in the universe, which means that there is five times more dark matter than visible matter. Most astronomers believe that dark matter is made up of subatomic particles called WIMPS (Weakly Interacting Massive Particles). These particles have not yet been discovered, although scientists suspect that they are present in the universe.

Physicists first theorized the existence of matter in 1933, in part because the dark manifestations showed that there was not enough observable matter in galaxies to keep them from falling apart

Today it is known that dark matter is made up of particles that do not absorb, reflect or emit light, so they cannot be detected by observing electromagnetic radiation. This, together with dark energy, makes up practically 95% of the universe. Astronomers were able to figure out where it is because it distorts the light from distant stars.
